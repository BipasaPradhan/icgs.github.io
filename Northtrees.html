Tree species found in each region are highly dependent on type and moisture. Soils in Northern Thailand are mostly very acidic. There are many forested areas where soils are rich in organic matter. Some common soils found in the region are Reddish Soil, Sandy Soil, Mountainous Soil, and many more. Because of this, the main types of trees that grow in this region are evergreen and deciduous.

Butea Monosperma
Common Name:
Flame of the Forest, Dhak
Thai Name:
Thong Gwao (ทองกวาว)
Ecological Niche:
Not common in the wild, usually in very degraded, fire-damaged areas. Frequently Planted for its flame-colored flowers. Best grown in sunny tropical locations, deep, fertile, well-drained soils in full sun. Needs consistent moisture, protected from strong winds.
Mammea Siamensis
Common Name:
Saraphi
Thai Name:
สารภี
Ecological Niche:
Often planted for its fragrant flowers, uncommon in less-disturbed forests. It is a tropical evergreen tree. est grown by seeds being sown in a well-drained, sandy soil mix and kept moist. Kept in a warm, humid environment and should be protected from direct sunlight.
Tectona Grandis
Common Name:
Teak
Thai Name:
Sak (สัก)
Ecological Niche:
Best grown in a sunny position, direct sunlight to the tree is recommended. The most suitable soil is deep, well-drained, fertile alluvial-colluvial soil with a pH of 6.5 ~ 8 in order for this tree to fully grow properly.
