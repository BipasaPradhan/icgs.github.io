In southern Thailand there are many areas that are tropical. Some soils that are commonly found are Lateritic soil, Alluvial soil, and Peat soil.

Mangrove Trees
Common Name:
Mangrove
Thai Name:
Ton Kong Kang (ต้นโกงกาง)
Ecological Niche:
A mangrove is a shrub or tree that grows mainly in coastal saline or brackish water. Mangroves grow in an equatorial climate, typically along coastlines and tidal rivers. Mostly found in Asia such as Indonesia, Thailand, and Malaysia.

(Photo by Prasongsom Punyauppa-path on Unsplash)

Durio
Common Name:
Durian Tree
Thai Name:
Ton Turian (ต้นทุเรียน)
Ecological Niche:
Durian cultivation thrives in tropical areas, ceasing growth when average daily temperatures fall below 22°C (72°F). The fruit is native to Southeast Asia and is commonly found in countries like Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, and the Philippines. Durian is often called the “king of fruits” in Southeast Asia due to its popularity and rich nutritions. Can be found in Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, and the Philippines.

Cocos nucifera
Common Name:
Coconut Palm
Thai Name:
Ton Maprow (ต้นมะพร้าว)
Ecological Niche:
Coconut palms flourish best close to the sea in low-lying areas a few feet above high water where there is circulating groundwater and ample rainfall. Coconut palms grow and crop best in a hot climate environment and the tree should get plenty of sunlight. Mostly found in Tropical coastal areas.
